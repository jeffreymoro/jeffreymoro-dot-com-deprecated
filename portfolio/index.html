Strange Creatures Made of Memory

For HASTAC’s (Humanities, Arts, Science, and Technology Alliance and Collaboratory) 2015 conference, “The Art and Science of Digital Humanities," I wrote an essay called "Strange Creatures Made of Memory," on glitch aesthetics and new possibilities for play. It's published at The Codex, an instance of The_Critical_Is.

The_Critical_Is

I write regularly for The_Critical_Is, a project I co-edit exploring new ways of teaching, writing, and thinking about video games and interactive narratives. You can check out my posts here; some of my favorite posts include: “On Gaming’s Willful Amnesia and “Liberating the Machine”.

Five College Digital Humanities Blog

I also write for Five College Digital Humanities’ ongoing blog, Sandbox.

Shakespeare Film and the Media of Translation

This is a longer piece that examines subtitles, intertitles, and other “media of translation” in a wide range of English and non-English Shakespeare film, and argues for renewed attention to the technologies that we use to translate media. It was my senior thesis in English, for which I received magna cum laude.

Lightning Talk: The Space of Mexico City

A lightning talk that I gave at Five College Digital Humanities’ 2014 Kickoff Celebration, about “The Space of Mexico City,” a project I assist on that’s building an interactive, experiential map of Mexico City’s lived environment.

Talk: Undergraduate Digital Humanities, Three Ways

I was invited to speak at a NERCOMP (Northeast Regional Computing Program) conference called “Emerging Digital Scholars: Undergraduates and Digital Humanities” on strategies Five College Digital Humanities uses to encourage student research and engagement with the digital humanities.

Video: Introduction to Twine

A tutorial video I made with Kimberly Bain on how to get started using Twine to make rudimentary text adventure games.

Video + Prezi: Five College Digital Humanities

This is video and interactive site that I designed and scripted in Prezi, exploring Five College Digital Humanities’ programs. I also wrote the video's script.

No Wind Blows Against the Pines

I wrote and directed No Wind Blows Against the Pines, adapted from two Noh plays, Pining Wind and The Sumida River. See photos here, credit to Julie Keresztes. Low-res performance video is on Vimeo. It was my senior thesis in Theater & Dance, for which I received magna cum laude.

Two Winds

Two Winds is a dance-theater piece I choreographed in collaboration with Skye Landgraf and Morgan Brown. It was a kind of first draft for No Wind Blows. See photos here, credit to Risalat Khan.

Shakespeare on the Quad Theater Company

I co-founded, produced, and occasionally acted for Shakespeare on the Quad Theater Company. We mounted free outdoor performances of Shakespeare comedies for the Pioneer Valley community. Photos will be available soon!
